Issue #1: The Origins

Born in Geneva, Rodolphe Töpffer had completely different goals in life when he was young. His father (Wolfgang Adam Töpffer) was a famous painter who started off poor, but climbed up the upper ranks od sociery with his skill. His dream was to live up to his father's progress, begining to paint from as early as possible. Shortly after applying to the same art-school like his father in Paris at the ripe age of 20, Rodolphe starts losing his eyesight. It worsened to the point that he had to drop out of school and go back to Geneva. To make money, he opens up a boarding school for young boys. To amuse them, he used his artistic skills and drew doodles for them, Making characters and putting them in silly stories. this time waster for Rodolph spurted the seed of a new medium that would later be known as a comic (book).

However, at the time, even though it was popular, artstyles like caricature was not nearly as respected as realism was, so Rodolphe wouldn't dare to publish them or show them to people who weren't his students or people were close to him until way later on with the help of Johann Wolfgang Von Goethe. Johan, like other famous artists, was depressed because of the french revolution, so one of his friends, who was a friend of Rodolph, kept bringing him his comic which brought a smile to his face. With his encouragement, Rodolphe overcomes his fear and manages to publish his first work! Although the critics tear him apart and by the time he published it, Johann had passed away, the public loves it so it becomes a huge hit, encouraging him to make more comics.

Issue #2: The Big Publishers

Marvel

back
The company began life as "Timely Publications" in 1939, with comic books featuring Captain America, Namor the Sub-Mariner and an early version of Human Torch. Legendary comics writer Stan Lee was hired as an office assistant in 1939. Within two years, Lee was promoted to editor of the Marvel Comics line, a post that he would keep until 1972. Everything changed in 1961, when Stan Lee and artist Jack Kirby ushered in "The Marvel Age of Comics" by creating characters which brought a new style of superhero comic that focused on the characters' internal drama as well as their heroic adventures. The style was a huge success.

DC

back
Wheeler-Nicholson's company pioneered the American comic book, publishing the first such periodical consisting solely of original material rather than reprints of newspaper comic strips, starting with Fun: The Big Comic Magazine #1, called New Fun after the first issue.It was also the first company to feature superheroes, beginning with Action Comics #1 in 1938. When the sales of the title proved unexpectedly strong and market research confirmed that the character, Superman, was the major reason, a period called the Golden Age of comic books began.

Image

back
It was founded in 1992 by seven high-profile illustrators as a venue where creators could publish their material without giving up the copyrights to the characters they created, as creator-owned properties. Image's success has significantly changed the position of creators in the comic book industry, but infighting between its partners and their lack of business experience have contributed to some volatile fortunes for the company. Nevertheless, the company is often the USA's third-largest comic book publisher.Its better-known series include Gen¹³, The Savage Dragon, Spawn, The Darkness , WildC.A.T.s, Cyberforce, Youngblood, ShadowHawk, and more recently Invincible.

Dark Horse

back
Dark Horse Comics is an American publishing company, and one of the largest independent American comic book publishers, behind Marvel Comics and DC Comics. Most well-known original titles include Hellboy, Sin City, The Mask, and 300. It has also held the rights to publish licensed comics based on existing media franchises such as Star Wars, Buffy the Vampire Slayer, Alien, Predator, and The Terminator. Dark Horse was the main publisher of Godzilla comics throughout the late 1980s and the 1990s, including the 17-issue Godzilla series.

IDW

back
The company was founded in 1999 and has been awarded the title "Publisher of the Year Under 5% Market Share" for the years 2004, 2005 and 2006 by Diamond Comic Distributors. IDW is the fifth largest publisher of American comic books. In addition to their printed comics and graphic novels, IDW has more than 650 books in digital distribution across multiple partners and platforms.

Issue #3: The Process

Step 1: Choose your canvas

It doesn't have to be a piece of paper, or a digital tablet. It can be carboard, it can be a piece of cement... It's your pick, you can make a story on anything!

Step 2: Select your tools

An artist's tools are like a boxer's gloves. Some are better than others, but ultimately, the outcome has nothing to do with them. If you're in the mood, you can take a marker and just draw on your leg even!

Step 3: Just do it

There are two types of people: People who do art and people who don't because they think they can't. It is one of the most subjective topic anywhere in world, it's just another skill that anyone can practice, and yet we assume that we don't have a permission to have fun with creating something without being talented. Why is that? Go ahead, make a fun story, make a terrible one, use pretty fonts, use ugly ones like the one I chose in the intro just because I felt like it. And you know what? I liked how It came out and I had fun!
